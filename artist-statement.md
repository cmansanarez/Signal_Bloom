Noir Mak
I create live generative systems that explore the evolving relationship between performer, machine, and emergence.

Working across Hydra, creative coding, AI image generation, immersive media, and live audiovisual performance, my practice investigates how digital systems can become unstable sites of transformation, resistance, and co-authorship. I am drawn to moments where control begins to collapse, where glitches behave like interruptions in dominant structures, and where new visual languages emerge through improvisation between human and machine intelligence.

My work often blends queer futurism, cybernetic aesthetics, abstraction, and signal corruption. Through live coding and generative feedback systems, I construct environments that feel simultaneously synthetic and emotional: shimmering noise fields, fractured geometries, recursive textures, distorted bodies, and evolving audiovisual landscapes that respond in real time.

I approach technology not as a neutral tool, but as a living system shaped by politics, identity, desire, labor, and power. By exposing process, instability, and machine behavior during performance, I aim to challenge fixed ideas of authorship and invite audiences into experiences that are dynamic, participatory, and continuously becoming.

Increasingly, my work centers on performative emergence: designing conditions where visuals are not fully predetermined, but instead evolve through interaction between code, generative systems, audience energy, sound, and improvisation. The resulting environments function as both speculative futures and temporary queer sanctuaries, spaces where collapse can become transformation and noise can become signal.
