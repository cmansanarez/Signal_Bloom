#Graduation Ceremony and A Working Title?

This week’s progress was a bit slower than usual as I spent much of the weekend traveling for graduation ceremonies and celebrations. Even so, I’m excited to say that the project has officially begun moving from conceptual development into technical implementation.

Over the past week, I started building the foundational development environment for the project in VS Code. At its core, the work is evolving into a modular AI-assisted live visual performance system designed to run locally on my machine. The long-term goal is to create an ecosystem where live coding, generative visuals, audio reactivity, and AI-generated imagery can interact dynamically during performance.

Right now, I’m focusing on the earliest technical layers of the project: establishing the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript architecture while integrating the Hydra video synth library as the foundation for live visual generation. Hydra is particularly exciting because it already contains powerful tools for real-time modulation, layering, recursion, feedback, and audio-reactive visuals. Leveraging these built-in functions gives me a strong starting point for experimentation.

One of the biggest conceptual developments this week has been realizing that the project does not necessarily require the most advanced AI model possible in order to succeed. Increasingly, I’m becoming more interested in emergence, transformation, and systems behavior than in photorealistic image generation itself. The project is less about creating perfect visuals and more about designing conditions where visuals evolve unpredictably through interaction between performer, machine systems, sound, and improvisation.

Visually, I’m drawn to balancing technological and organic language. I want the environments to feel simultaneously cybernetic and alive, synthetic but emotional. A lot of the work is beginning to orbit around themes of emergence, mutation, recursion, instability, and transformation.

I’m also beginning conversations with the program director regarding possible implementation strategies involving ComfyUI and local AI generation workflows. At this stage, I’m trying to better understand how local image or video generation systems could integrate into a live coding environment while remaining technically achievable within the scope of the capstone.

As the conceptual direction continues to evolve, I’ve started exploring a working title for the project:

##Signal Bloom

The title refers to the moment when digital systems evolve beyond static control and enter states of emergence, mutation, and unpredictable growth.

In signal processing, a signal represents the transmission of information, energy, or communication across systems. A bloom suggests organic expansion, transformation, and becoming. Together, the phrase reflects the central thesis of the project:

**the evolving relationship between performer, machine, and emergence.**

At its core, the project explores how live code, AI generation, sound, and human improvisation can interact to produce audiovisual environments that continuously evolve beyond predetermined authorship. Increasingly, I’m beginning to think of the system itself as the artwork rather than any single generated output.

My next steps will likely involve creating mood boards, visual studies, and interface sketches to begin refining the project’s visual language and environmental aesthetic. I want to identify recurring forms, textures, symbols, and color systems that support the themes of queer futurism, cybernetic emergence, glitch aesthetics, and performative transformation.

The project still feels fluid and highly experimental, but for the first time it feels like the technical systems and artistic concepts are beginning to align into a cohesive direction.
